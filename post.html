ROBOTIC

What is Robotic?

Robotic is the intersection of science, engineering and technology that produces machines, called robots, that substitute for (or replicate) human actions.
In 2005, 90% of all robots could be found assembling cars in automotive factories. These robots consist mainly of mechanical arms tasked with welding or screwing on certain parts of a car.
Today, we're seeing an evolved and expanded definition of robotics that includes the development, creation and use of bots that explore Earth's harshest conditions, robots that assist law-enforcement and even robots that assist in almost every facet of healtcare.


Types of robots

Mechanical bots come in all shapes and sizes to efficiently carry out the task for which they are designed. From the 0.2 millimeter-long "RobobBee" to the 200 meter-long robotic shipping vessel "Vindskip", robots are emerging to carry out tasks that humans simply can't.
Generally, there are five types of robots:

  1. Pre-Programmed Robots
  2. Humandroid Robots
  3. Autonomous Robots
  4. Teleoperated Robots
  5. Augmenting Robots
